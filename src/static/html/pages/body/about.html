About

Timeline Trivia is an open source online multiplayer party game.

It's a fork of Card Judge by GrantFBarnes — credit and thanks to the original project for the platform this was built on.

Feel free to donate if you want to help support development/hosting — this donation link goes to the maintainer of Card Judge, not this fork.

Definitions

A card is a historical event with a year it took place.

A deck is a grouping of cards.

A lobby is an active game taking place.

A draw pile is the available cards within a lobby.

A timeline is a player's own row of correctly-placed cards, kept in chronological order.

A user is an account created on this site.

A player is a user in a lobby.

Rules
Start Playing

To start playing, create/join a lobby and select at least one deck. A lobby can optionally be restricted to one or more year ranges, limiting the draw pile to cards whose year falls within those ranges.

Playing a Turn

The current event card is displayed for all players to read, with its year hidden. On your turn, place it into your own timeline where you believe it belongs relative to the events you've already placed.

If the placement is correct, the card joins your timeline in its proper chronological position, and the next player to start a turn draws a new card.

If it's incorrect, the same card is instead offered to the next player, who can try to steal it into their own timeline. A dummy card marks where each player guessed wrong until the card is resolved, so everyone can see what's already been tried. This continues around the table until someone places it correctly or every player has guessed wrong, in which case the card is discarded. Either way, the turn to start the next card always passes to the next player after whoever started this one — regardless of who ultimately won or lost the steal.

Play continues until a player's timeline reaches the number of cards required to win or the draw pile is exhausted.

Winning

The first player to correctly place enough cards to reach the lobby's configured "cards to win" wins the game.
